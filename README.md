## üåü Core Philosophy: The Anti-Algorithm Standard

A website is deemed "Cognitively-Kind" only if it adheres to these principles:

1.  **NO Algorithm:** Content is delivered based on **explicit user choice** (e.g., following a link, visiting a static category page, or chronologically) and **never** via a proprietary feed, "For You" page, or recommendation engine designed to maximize time-on-site.
2.  **Human-Generated, currated as much as possible:** Content is verifiably **created and maintained by human thought and effort, not complete AI slop**.
3.  **Low-Distraction Design:** Minimalist layout, no intrusive ads, no auto-play media, and a complete absence of infinite scrolling.
4.  **High-Signal, Low-Noise:** Content is substantial, educational, or genuinely interesting, free from sensationalism, manufactured outrage, or clickbait.

## üìö Deep Reading & Essays (Anti-Feed)

Thought-provoking, well-written essays and long-form content that you seek out deliberately.

* [**The Marginalian**](https://www.themarginalian.org/) - *Maria Popova's timeless reflections on art, science, philosophy, and poetry. Articles are organized by date or subject, not a feed.*
* [**Kottke.org**](https://kottke.org/) - *One of the oldest blogs, sharing curated links to fascinating, high-quality content. Content is chronological and hand-picked.*
* [**Wait But Why**](https://waitbutwhy.com/) - *Extremely long, hand-illustrated posts that meticulously break down complex topics (e.g., AI, space, history). Human-drawing and deep research ensure content is not AI-slop.*
* [**Ribbonfarm**](https://www.ribbonfarm.com/) - *Deep, long-form blog on management, meaning, and technology written by Venkat Rao. Content is organized chronologically, encouraging deliberate consumption.*
* [**Zen Habits**](https://zenhabits.net/) - *Leo Babauta's simple, minimalist blog on achieving a peaceful life through simplicity and mindfulness. Extremely clean, free from distractions.*

## üí¨ Community & Discussion (Structured Forums)

Platforms that use traditional message board structures, prioritizing chronological discussion and human-generated content over personalized feeds.

* [**Hacker News (YC)**](https://news.ycombinator.com/) - *A simple, text-based discussion board focused on technology, startups, and human thought. Comments are purely chronological, heavily filtered for substance, and not a personalized feed.*
* [**Physics Forums**](https://www.physicsforums.com/) - *A massive, highly moderated, serious science and engineering discussion community. Content is organized by topic and date, not algorithm, ensuring high-signal focus.*
* [**LinuxQuestions**](https://www.linuxquestions.org/) - *A classic, plain-text forum for supporting the Linux operating system. Focus is technical problem-solving and human collaboration in chronological threads.*
* [**The Straight Dope Message Board**](https://boards.straightdope.com/) - *Features extensive, in-depth, human-written debates and research on history, language, and science. Strictly moderated for logic and substance.*
